Requirements

Project Background

This is a project with Junaid Saleem Malik of the UCL Eastman Dental Institute. We were presented with the utility of clinical decision support systems to provide systematic and unified decision-making for treating dental disease.

This has been explored and implemented quite widely within the field of prosthodontics. However, this technology has yet to break into the prosthodontic management of tooth wear due to the number of varying treatment approaches present. There are even several indices to grade tooth wear.

Client Requirements

After recieving our client brief and familiarising ourselves with the project background, we set up an initial meeting with our client to better understand what they expect from the project and the overall main goals of the project. We also agreed upon a weekly meeting to ensure everyone is up to date with the progress of the project and recieve regular feedback.

After the initial meeting, we had some clear ideas of what our client expects of the project. Below are the main requirements they pointed out:

  • Take in 3D tooth models in STL/PLY file format
  • Store patient data as input
  • Run analysis on 3D tooth models
  • Provide diagnosis and prognosis suggestions to dentists

Project Goals

The overarching goal of this project is to develop a windows based app that can work as a Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) by providing dentists with treatment plan suggestions based on an input of a 3D tooth scan and patient metadata.

This should include capabilities such as: rendering of STL/PLY files, storage of tooth models and patient data in a database, an Artificial Intelligence based engine for grading tooth wear and suggesting treatment plans. The app itself should also have a straightforward UI that will reduce the amount of friction in work for dentists who would be using the app regularly.

User Interviews

To get a better understanding of more specific features and behaviours the app should contain, we had to conduct user interviews. After considering various methods, we decided the most suitable approach would be a semi-structured interview with straight-forward and open-ended questions.

This was done with all three team members and an interviewee over teams in an informal setting. This seemed optimal as such a dialogue would be less restrictive to the user's point of view which is crucial as it is precisely their perspective we are aiming to understand better.

  • Is there already any similar software out there?

    • There is some work done but only with 2D scans and its very technical. Otherwise, only some academic work.

  • Are there any specific functionalities you have in mind?

    • Software should be able to tag/segment teeth and suggest a level of tooth wear.

    Decision making support capabilities should be based on the level of tooth wear and some patient metadata.

    PLY file format is preferred over STL as it preserves the colour of the model which is useful when classifying tooth wear as discolouration is an important indicator.

  • What should the ‘tagging’ aspect of the software behave like?

    • Software should identify teeth and tag/highlight areas of the teeth, whether that is volumetric loss, prosthetic, discolouration… Then the software should provide a clinician with information useful for treatment plan development.

  • How will this software assist clinicians?

    • From the perspective that the app is fully delivered, it will aid in diagnosis of tooth wear, help develop prognosis, and assist in suggesting treatment plans. This could also potentially expand to other fields in medicine where analysis of 3D models is similarly pertinent.
