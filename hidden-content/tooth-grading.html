Tooth Grading Algorithm (ML & DL)

Research in deep learning with tooth evaluation

The main algorithms that have been used in academia for tooth evaluation are classification, segmentation, and grading algorithms. The classification and segmantation algorithms are usually used to identify individual teeth and classify whether they are incisors, canines, premolars, or molars. However, the issue with such algorithms is that they are very specialised for 3D tooth models. Neither the patient dataset or algorithms are open source let alone optimised for industrial use. Therefore, we had to settle for a more general purpose architecture with proven performence on a public dataset.

Research in Point Cloud

As mentioned in the client requirements, the input of the Deep Learning (DL) model is in the PLY format. To be more specific, it is a point cloud which consists of thousands of points with x, y, and z coordinates. This figure9 shows an overview of the related research in point cloud DL models, and we mainly focused on the classification (highlighted in red). This is most relevant to our project as we can transfer the classification model to a regression model for tooth grading.

One such model which belongs to PointWise MLP Methods is the PointNet8 neural network. The PointNet model solved two key issues caused by the properties of point cloud data:

  • Permutation Invariance (Unordered): the oder of points should not affect results
  • Transformation Invariance: rotating and translating points should not affect results

PointNet achieves a state-of-the-art performance on some public datasets (eg. ModelNet40). It also proves its robustness on deleting points, inserting outliers, and adding Gaussian noise.
