Tooth Grading Algorithm (ML & DL)

Research in deep learning with tooth evaluation

The main algorithms that have been used in academia for tooth evaluation are classification and segmentation. Then followed by the actual grading algorithm. The classification and segmantation algorithms are usually used to identify individual tooth and then classify whether the tooth is a incisors, canines, premolars, or molars. The figure7 blow shows an example of the proces:

However, the issue with such algorithms is that they are very specialised particularly for 3D tooth models. Neither of the patient dataset and algorithms are not open source and optimised for industrial use yet. Therefore, we had to settle for a more general purpose architecture with proven excellent performence on public dataet.

Research in Point Cloud

As mentioned in the client requirements, the input of the DL model is in PLY format and to be more specific, it is a point cloud which consists of thousands of points with x, y, and z coordinate. This figure9 shows an overview of the related research in point cloud, and we mainly focused on the classification (shown in red box) which is the most relevant to our project as we can transfer the classification model to regression model for tooth grading.

One such model which belongs to PointWise MLP Methods is the PointNet8 neural network. This pioeering work better solved problems related to the two main property of point cloud data:

  • Permutation Invariance (Unordered): the oder of points should not affect results
  • Transformation Invariance: rotating and translating points should not affect results

PointNet achieves a state-of-the-art performance on some publict dataset (eg. ModelNet40) among other popular deep learning networks. It also proves its robustness on deleting points, inserting outliers, and adding Gaussian noise.
