Week 7: Literature Review Pt 2 (Nov 21 - Nov 27)

Continuing our literature review we had to consider some practical implications of the research we were doing. The main one being that the complex AI architectures that we found online have not been implemented in software and are not optimised. So although, they have promising results it is unlikely we would be able to replicate the results within an app (given the time frame for the project) and even if we could they would be too computationally taxing for an everyday app. Hence the model would require extensive optimisation.

Therefore, we had to look into more practical and implementable solutions for algorithms we could use for tooth grading. After some looking around at more general models we could tailor to tooth wear grading we found PointNet. A robust neural-network architecture specifically made for classification and segmentation of 3D point-cloud models. The general structure can bee seen below.

Due to its widespread popularity there was much previous open-source work we could look towards for implementing a tooth grading algorithm. All we had to do was tailor it for tooth wear analysis which was a far simpler task compared to recreating the highly complex multi-model algorithm from most academic papers.

Another import issue to note is data gathering. It has been something we have struggled with much as there are virtually no 3D PLY models of teeth online. Since this is a very new technology that hasn't been fully accepted yet. Most tooth scans are 2D and those which are three dimensional are usally STL files which do not encode color. All we could do is liaise with our client and hope he could provide us with some. Our client would now have to produce these scans from prosthetic 'fake' teeth which would be costly and much time to would be needed for them to arrive. Alternatively get scans from real people, but this requires long legal processes to even begin doing.
