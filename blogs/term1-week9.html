We read a paper about a highly accurate three-dimensional (3D) superimposition technique has been suggested recently for occlusal tooth wear assessment. The paper concludes that using the complete crown as reference area, with reduced estimated overlap of meshes to 20%, provide the most accurate outcome when calculating the volume loss of tooth structure. This paper mentions a software call WearCompare which is an application that can calculate volume loss of tooth structure by inputting two tooth scans taken at different time points.

WearCompare

Wear Compare, developed by University of Leeds, is a purpose-built surface matching freeware designed for dental applications. It can be used for quantifying tooth wear progression as it outputs the volume loss of tooth structure when the user inputs two tooth scans of the same patient taken at different point of time. The underlying software registration algorithm of this application includes feature based global registration coupled with a custom iteratively refined version of the Iterative Closest Point algorithm. This app is part of our technology review, it is the first app we found that is designed specifically for tooth wear purposes. The advantage of this app is that it is free for usage and provides accurate results when quantifying tooth wear progression. The drawback of this app is its functionally is quite limited.

WearCompare vs the app we want to develop

The app that we are going to develop will focus more on using machine learning or deep learning to calculate tooth wear degree. Instead of having the percentage of tooth loss being outputted, we aim to output the tooth wear grade, totalling of 5 grades. Besides, users of WearCompare is expected to provide tooth scans of the same patient in different time points. For the app that we are going to develop, users just need to input the current tooth scan of patient and the tooth wear grade of patient will be displayed. Besides, our app aim to provide more functionalities such as analysing the cause of tooth wear (mechanical tooth wear, chemical tooth wear and pathological tooth wear).
