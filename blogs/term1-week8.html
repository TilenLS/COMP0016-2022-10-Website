Week 8: Literature Review Pt 3 (Nov 28 - Dec 4)

Now that we had covered the base part of the algortihm, grading tooth wear, this had to be incorporated to a parent algorithm that would use those results to provide clinical decision support.

After reviewing several papers on Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS), some specific to dentsitry and others more general we had a good overview of how they can be structured.

One obvious solution would be to use another neural network that takes the tooth wear grades and patient data as input and suggests treatment plans. Given enough training data this would potentially provide very accurate results. However, this again would require our client to produce data of tooth scans of real patients with their personal and medical backgrounds, along with the corresponding dentist recommended treatment plans.

Even though this has been included in our research it will not be included within the development of the project due to time constraints. This will likely be done by later generations who get handed down this project.

An interesting side note. Another similar proposal for a CDSS is to use a neuro-fuzzy system. This combines the human reasoning of fuzzy logic with the human learning aspect of neural networks. There are few examples of this approach having been used to implement CDSS with very promising results. However, this is primarily academic work and very hard to realistically implement.
